01

Overview

The short version: TabNest stores everything locally on your device. No data is ever transmitted to any server. We collect nothing about you.

TabNest is a browser extension that automatically organizes your tabs into smart groups and manages them through a 4-stage lifecycle to reduce RAM usage and clutter. This policy explains what data the extension touches, where it lives, and what we do — and do not do — with it.

TabNest is not affiliated with Google, Mozilla, Microsoft, or any browser vendor.

02

Permissions & Why We Request Them

TabNest requests the following browser permissions. Each is used exclusively for the feature listed — nothing more.

tabs Read each tab's URL, title, and favicon to build the sidebar view and drive the lifecycle pipeline. This data stays in memory and local storage only.
storage Persist tab snapshots, group metadata (names, colors, order), and user preferences in chrome.storage.local and chrome.storage.sync on your device.
alarms Schedule a 30-second background tick that advances tabs through lifecycle stages (Active → Discarded → Saved → Archived) without requiring an open UI.
sidePanel Render the TabNest sidebar panel alongside your browser window. Used by Chrome, Edge, and Brave (Manifest V3).
webNavigation Capture a tab's navigation history (up to 20 URLs) before the tab is closed, so it can be fully restored later.
activeTab Identify the currently active tab so it is never automatically discarded or closed by the lifecycle pipeline.
03

Data Collected

TabNest processes the following data locally in order to function. None of it is "collected" in the sense of leaving your device.

  • Tab URLs, titles, and favicons — used to display tabs in the sidebar and classify them into groups.
  • Tab lifecycle metadata — timestamps recording when a tab was last active, created, saved, or archived.
  • Navigation history — up to 20 URLs per tab, captured before closure to enable full session restore.
  • Group configuration — names, colors, ordering, and domain rules you define.
  • User preferences — lifecycle thresholds, keyboard shortcuts, and display settings.
  • Workspace snapshots — point-in-time saves of your entire tab layout (up to 20 snapshots).

TabNest does not collect your name, email address, IP address, device identifiers, or any form of personal profile.

04

How Data Is Stored

All data is stored exclusively through the browser's built-in storage APIs:

  • chrome.storage.local (up to 10 MB) — tab snapshots, saved entries, navigation history, workspace snapshots, and session checkpoints. This data never leaves your device.
  • chrome.storage.sync (up to 100 KB) — user preferences and group metadata. This syncs between your own signed-in browser instances via Google or Mozilla sync infrastructure. TabNest has no access to that sync channel — the browser handles it entirely.
No external servers. TabNest has no backend, no API endpoint, no database, and makes no network requests of any kind. The extension operates entirely offline within your browser.
05

Data Sharing & Third Parties

TabNest does not share any data with any third party. Specifically:

  • No analytics or usage tracking (no Google Analytics, Mixpanel, or similar).
  • No telemetry or crash reporting sent to any remote service.
  • No advertising networks or data brokers.
  • No remote configuration or feature-flag services.
  • No content delivery networks — all extension assets are bundled locally.

The extension source code is available in its repository for independent verification of these claims.

06

Your Control Over Data

You have full control over all data TabNest stores:

  • Delete individual entries — remove any saved or archived tab directly from the sidebar.
  • Clear all data — use the Settings page within TabNest to wipe all local storage at once.
  • Uninstall — removing the extension from your browser deletes all associated storage data automatically.
  • Disable sync — turn off syncing for extensions in your browser's account settings to prevent chrome.storage.sync from replicating across devices.
